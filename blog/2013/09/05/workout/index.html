to buy

  • belt (olympic 2” or ppowerlifting belt 4” )
  • wraps. wrist wraps (24”)

  • one day will be devoted to the standing military press, one day to the parallel squat, one day to the deadlift and one day to the bench press.

  • each training cycle lasts 4-6 weeks. This depends on how many days a week you train.

  • the first week you will do 3 sets of 5 reps (3x5)

  • the second week you will do 3 sets of 3 reps (3x3)
  • the third week you will do 1 set of 5 reps, 1 set of 3 reps and 1 set of 1 rep (5/3/1)
  • after the fourth week, you begin again with 3 sets of 5 reps.
  • each week and each set has a percentage to follow, so you won’t be guessing what to do.

First, know your maxes for the four lifts

  • squat
  • bench
  • deadlift
  • standing military press

How? Take 80%-85% of your supposed max and perfom as many reps ass possible. Then plug the reps and the weight into this formula to get your estimated 1RM:

Weight * Reps * 0.0333 + Weight = Estimated 1RM

Take 90% of this number and use this as your ”max” for the first 4 weeks of the training cycle. For example, let’s say I have a 300 deadlift, 220 squat, 180 bench press and 100 military press, then my numbers would look like this:

Deadlift: 300 * 0.9 = 270 Squat: 220 * 0.9 = 198 Bench: 180 * 0.9 = 162 Military press: 100 * 0.9 = 90

Week 1

65% * 5 reps, 75% * 5 reps, 85% * 5 or more reps

Week 2

70% * 3 reps, 80% * 3 reps, 90% * 3 or more reps

Week 3

75% * 5 reps, 85% * 3 reps, 95% * 1 or more reps

Week 4

40% * 5 reps, 50% * 5 reps, 65% * 5

Warming up

1 * 5 40% 1 * 5 50% 1 * 3 60% Worksets

When you get sick, go on vacation, etc what to do?

  • start where you left off and only get prescribed reps.
  • do the deload week first to get back into the swing of things

What to do if you miss a training day?

  • just pick up where you left off.
