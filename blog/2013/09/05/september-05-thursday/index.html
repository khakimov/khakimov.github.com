How should we live? We could do it wrong. Mortality rubs our chance do-over.

We have to be careful in our aims and we have to be careful in our execution of our aims, because we have a rather limited amount of time to do it over.

We just don’t have enought time to flail around, try a little of this, try a little of that. And so what death forces us to do is to be careful. The face about our death requires paying attention.

Ok, I’m paying attention, so what should I do with my life? What should I fill it with? What kinds of contents should we try to fill our lives with?

Strategy number one says given that you’ve only got a finite amount of time, pack as much as you can into life. Given the dangers of failure if you aim too ambitiously, you should settle for the kinds of goals that you’ve virtually guaranteed that you’ll accomplish. The pleasure of food, company, sex, ice cream. Eat, drink, and be merry, for tomorrow we die.

Strategy number two says that’s all well and good, you’ve got a pretty high chance of succeeding at that. The trouble with strategy number one is the goods that you can achive are small. You might want to write a novel, compose a symphony or marry and raise a family.
