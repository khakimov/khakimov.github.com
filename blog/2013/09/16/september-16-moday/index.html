Building Pyramids

In order to build large cities, kindgoms and empires, more food is of course a necessary condition but it is not a sufficient condition. The mere fact that you can feed 10000 people in the same town or a million doesnt guarantee that they can agree how to divide this food, land and water, how to settle disputes and conflicts. And how to act in times of crisis like drought (засуха) or war.

Sapiens had to rely on inventing all kinds of stories, new imagined realities that could regulate cooperation on such a huge scale. Farmers began to weave stories that eventually enable millions of strangers to cooperate together. If we go back to around 8500 BC, we will find that the largest settlements in the world were villages such as Jercho which could take a few hundres invidiuals at most. By 3000 BC, you could already find cities and kindoms ruling tens of thousands and even hundreds of thousands of people. And then Roman empire in the Mediterranean region, and the Han empire in China (millions of people). This cities, kindoms and empires were based on the belief on common emergent realities.

Imagined order - means that the social and legal norms, that sustained these kindoms and empires, and kept their society stable, were base not on some natural instincts and not on personal acquaintance, rather they were based on belief in share stories, shared imagined realities.

Arguments Markers

I am tall, and I am good at sport.

vs

I am tall, so I am good at sports.

When you use the word and, you’re simply stating the two facts. But when you use the word so (therefore, thus, hence, accordingly), you’re indicating that one of them is a reson for the other.

Kinds of argument markers:

Conclusion markers: so, therefore, thus, accordingly, they indicate that the sentece right after them is a conclusion.

After the words because, for, as, for the reason that, and the reason why are a reason or a premise. And we going to call it a reason marker, or a premise marker.
