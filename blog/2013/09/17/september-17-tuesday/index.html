[Modernart] Intensity and the Ordinary [Humankind] Building Pyramids

Freud established his credibility as an enlightenment thinker, he goes on to talk about why the only thing you can really look at scienitifcally is the pleasure principle. Three sources of suffering - own body, the external world and other people.

He says, so many other writers, whether they’re religious writers or philosophical writers or politicals, social commentators, what they really want to do is instead of facing what really causes us sorrow, and pain, they want actually to give us consolation (утешение). Consolation is not what we should pursue, it is awful thing, because it disguises where our suffering comes from and makes it harder for us to deal with these sources of suffering

Life, as we find it, is too hard for us; it brings us too many pains, disappointments and impossible tasks. In order to bear it we cannot dispense with palliative (паллиативный, смягчающий) measures. Civilization and Its Discontents

It means we come up with some kind of drugs, so we don’t have to deal with life as it really is.

… we cannot fail to be struck by the similarity between the process of civiliation and the libidinal (sexual desire) development of the individual. … it is impossible to overlook the extent to which civilization is built upon a renunciation (formal rejection of something) of instinct, how much it presupposes precisely the non-satisfaction… of powerful instincts.

Every time you have people say, well, love your neighbor, be to strangers, all that, you always find some scapegoat (a person who is blamed for the wrongdoing, mistakes козел отпущения) that you can take your rage out on. Every time you ask people to be sweet and kind as a rule, they will find something to attack.

In this respect the Jewish people, scattered everywhere, have rendered the most useful service to the civiliations of the countries that have been their hosts; but unfortunately all the massacres of the Jews in the Middle Ages did not suffice to make that period more peaceful and secure for their Christian fellows. Civilization and Its Discontents.

Not only do we find scapegoats out there that we can hate and attack and turn our aggression inward as a result of our guilt. As we become more civilized we have more and more occasions to feel we’re not living up to our ideals, become twisted and wracked by guilt, because we’re not living up to the expectations we’ve created for ourselves. We’re not studying enough, we’re not working hard enought, we’re not making enought money.
