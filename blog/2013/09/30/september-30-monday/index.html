[Modernart] the Postmodern Everyday

Two person who were important for contemporary philosophy Ralph Waldo Emerson and Ludwin Wittgenstein. Emerson is a key figure in American intellectual history, cultural history, but until pretty recently wasn’t seen as a key figure in philosophy. Often flowery (витиеватый) writer, he is a writer of wonderful sentences one of the most quotable authors in American history. Emerson’s philosophical contribution to American culture as an author whose importance rivals that of Nietzsche and of Heidegger both of whom were influenced by Emerson.

Emerson was born during the presidency of Thoman Jefferson, so very early in the nineteenth century, lived a long life, he was at some point a minister and left the pulpit, because of a crisis of faith, not so much in the divine being but in instritutionalized religion and became an eassayst and lecturer. Emerson had audiences of thousands of people around the contry who were following his intellectual excursions via lectures in towns across the United States. His departure from enlightenment tradition as it had been imported into the United Stated is really key.

The englightenment tradition in the United States was very much indebted to the Lockian Tradition, that is the view that all our ideas come from sensations that we are receptacle of stimuli from the world and that our ideas and then our belief and our work comes as we process the stimulation, the sensation that comes into us. In some ways, thinking of us as a blank slate and having these impressions made upon us by the world. That’s the tradition that the American progressive thinkes the beginning of the 19th century adopted.

Emerson has a different view, his view is kind of an activist mind, for him, experience isn’t just receiving the world. Emerson’s view is that the mind goes out into the world, we make our experience in an active way (something there from Kant). Experience comes not by just humans being receptacles, but humans creating experience by actively living in the world.

If tomorrow I should be informed of the bankruptcy of my principal debtors, the loss of my property would be a great inconvenience to me, perhaps, for many years; but it would leave me as it found me, — neither better nor worse. Life is a train of moods like a string of beads, and, as we pass through them, they prove to be many-colored lenses which paint the world their own hue, and each shows only what lies in its focus.

There are times in our lives when we think that we are living a kind of banal existence. Everything is routine, that you know there, we go to work everyday or we go to school everyday, we have our meals at the same time, we see the same members of our family, our friends, our spouses, our lovers, whatever we’re depending on our situation. And after while we’ve realized we’re not feeling things. It’s just going thorought life, going through the motions we might say. And sometimes we want a crisis, you want something big to happen, even if it’s a bad thing, because it makes feel more alive, more vital.

If you had some crisis, if you had a grief, you would at least wake up to the world, because you’re living in the world without waking up to it. But grief will make us idealists. There are times when we think that we are away from the world and we hope that some huge even happens, even a horrible event, that will make us at least, feel the world.

But in fact, it’s a mistate to think you need some huge experience, some trauma, to wake you up to the world.

We animate what we can, and we see only what we animate.

We make the world alive to us. We enliven the world.
