[Humankind] History's Biggest Fraud. Part 2 and 3 [Thinkagain] What Are Arguments Used For?

In this part we’re going to talk about the question why people move towards wheat? It’s not offer people economic security, not good for the diet, because has less mineral and vitamins, hard to digest and bad for our teeth. The life of a peasant is usually less secure than the life of a hunter gatherer, they gather, hunt and eat a lot of of different food.

Chance of the men to die at the hand of another man was 25% in ancient agricultural village say 8 000 years ago. Today in the world it’s about 1.5% - 1%, even less.

Cultivating wheat provided much more food per unit of territory for a given field. Homo sapiens could get much more food if we used agriculture than if we hunted and gather what frows naturally in that field. Extra food enable the number of Sapiens to grow exponentially.

Evolutionary success of a specie is measured only by the number of copies of DNA in existence. Because of extra food, villages and connection to the one place woman could have a child every year instead of having baby just every three or four years as was the case with hunter gatherers (woman were very careful to wait until the previous child could walk before having another baby).

Moreover, infectious diseases began to spread rapidly (from domesticated animals to people, problems with toilet and garbage piles, diet was worse and immune system was less robust)

Population kept growing and growing all the time, and therefore, even if people wanted to go back from agruculture back to the hunting and gathering, they can’t because they have much more people to feed. So they kept watering the wheat firlds and feeding all “extra” people.

The disire to have a better and easier life actually treapped human kind in harder living conditions.

Luxuries tend to become necessities. Once people get used to a certain habit, they begin to count on it and to take it for granted and eventually they reach the position when they can’t live without it.

Example with email vs post mail. It obviously very good and easy to be able to send something and get response very fast versus sending the message with envelope and stamps on it and get the answer in weeks or even month. The life of people who use emails is not more easy and more relaxed than the life of people who used only letters. Humanity search for an easier life, releases immense forces of change which tranform the world in ways that nobody envisioned or wanted. It’s extremely difficult to foresee all the results of our actions and all the factors which in order to make really good decisions, we need to tak into account.

Alternative explanation about agricultural revolution. It suggests that what brought about the agricultural revolution was not the search for an easier and more comfortable life, rather, it was the attempt to fulfil some kind of cultural or religious aspiration.

What are Arguments used for?

Persuading is making people believe or do something that they would not otherwise believe or do.

Justifying is showing someone a reason to believe the conclusion.

Justification tries to give good reasons. Persuasion tries to change your belief or actions (and bad reasons can do that).

When someone give you an argument, you need to ask a series of questions:

  1. Is the arguer trying to change someone’s mind? If so, the purpose is persuasion.

  2. Is the arguer trying to give some kind of reason to believe something? If so, the purpose is justification.
