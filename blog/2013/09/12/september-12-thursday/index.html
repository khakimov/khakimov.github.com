Strong arguments don’t always persuade everyone

Religious and cultural beliefs can be too hard to overcome. So even the best arguments can have disbelievers. Human beings aren’t always logical and don’t always believe scientifically proven cause and effect.

A valid, strong and sound argument in and of itself may never persuade or convert anyone to adpt a different way of thinking. So what. What a strong argument does is to communicate clearly what one think and why they think it.

Don’t set your sights too high. If your goal is to persuade everybody, you’re going to be constantly frustrated.

What else Arguments used for? Explanation

You can use Arguments in order to explain things. Explaining is gigivng a reason why something happened (or why it is true).

When you justify something, your audience might not believe what you are justifying.

When you explain something, you and your audience both believe what you are explaining.

The goal of explanation is to help people understan why something is true.

Kinds of Explanations:

  • Causal: Why did the bridge collapse? The earthquake shook it.
  • Teleological (purpose): Why did Joe go to the grocery store? To buy milk.
  • Formal: Why doesn’t this peg fit in that round hole? The peg is square.
  • Material: Why is this golf club light? It is made of graphite.
