[Death] Believing You Will Die

We don’t have P-functioning when we sleep, so should we say we dead then? Well, that’s clearly not the right thing to say.

What is it to be dead?

It can’t be just a matter of not P-functioning. Suppose that to be alive is to be able to engage in P-functioning.

Sometimes people say that we die alone or everybody dies alone. Somehow, at some level, nobody really believes they’re going to die at all. Since it’s impossible to picture being dead, nobody believes in the possibility that they’re going to die, that they’re going to cease to exist.

What is it like to be dead? We can’t imagine being dead. But can we imagine being cell phone or bold ball pen? No, so, there’s no mystery about what it’s like to be dead - it isn’t like anything. There is nothing there to describe. When you’re dead there’s nothing happening on the inside to be imagined.

From the mere fact that you can’t picture from the inside what it’s like to be dead, it doesn’t follow that nobody believes they’re going to die.

You can not picture it from the inside, but you can picture it from the outside. Dreamless or fainting - picture of my body lying on the bed or ground unconscious. I can picture my being dead quit easily, no functioning occuring in my body.

Everytime I try to picture myself being dead, I smuggle myself back in, conscious and existing as a person, hence, not dead as a person. Maybe my body (i imagining my body dead, but i’m not imagining myself, the person, dead).

Nobody believes they’re going to undergo bodily death?
