Nutrition Digestion and Absorption

Gastrointestinal tract is responsible for receiving food:

  • receives food: takes food from mouth
  • separates nutrients from foods: releasing them in stomach
  • transports nutrients via blood: microvilli in small intestine transfer nutrients into portal blood
  • excretes indigestible waste: fecal waste

Digestive system

  • mouth
  • pharynx
  • salivary glands
  • esophagus
  • liver (bile is made. bile is important in helping to digest and absorb fat)
  • stomach (secrete (выделяют) acids and protein-digesting enzimes)
  • pancreas (releases a number of different hormones and bicarbonate. Bicarbonate is to help neutralize the intestinal contents, neytralizing acid content, also produces enzymes that digest carbohydrate, protein, and fat.)
  • gallbladder (stores bile, and then relases it into the small intestine when it’s needed)
  • small intestine (responsible for absorbing nutrients into the blood or the lymph. most of the nutritions absobed)
  • large intestine (water and vitamines and minerals absorbed. it’s home to bacteria that help ferment some of the fibers that you eat)
  • anus (waste)

The Stomach

Esophagus -> Lower esophageal sphincter -> Fundus -> Pyloric sphincter

Spincter helps to keep acid inside the stomach. Primarty nutrients absorbed - water, alcohol, minerals. Highly acidic enviroment and it secretes a lot of gastric juices:

  • hydrochloric acid (denatures protein and activate pepsin)
  • pepsin (enzume that digest protein)
  • gastric lipase (enzume that digest fat)
  • mucus ( protects stomach lining)

Small Intestine

  • main site for digestion and absorption
  • chyme moved by peristalsis and mixed by rhythmic constractions called segmentation
  • 20 feet long
  • undigested pass from small to large intestine through a sphinchter

Parts of the small intestine:

  • Duodenum: minerals (calcium, phsphorus, magnesium, iron, copper) and vitamins (thamin, riboflavin, niacin, biotin, folate, A,D,E,K)
  • Jejunum: minerals (calcuymm phowphous, magnesium, iron, zinc, chromium, mangenese, molybdenum) vitamins: thiamin, riboflavin, niacin, pantothenate, biotin, folate, B6, C, A,D,E,K; lipids; carbohydrates: monosaccharides; protins: amino acids and small peptides
  • Ileum: minerals (magnesium), vitamin C, B12, D, K, folate, bile salts and acids

The large intestine

  • made up of the colon and rectum
  • 5 feet long
  • water, some vitamins and minerals are absorbed here
  • intestinal microflora break down fiber, synthesize small amount of B vitamins, K vitamin
  • from colon, contents pass to rectum where stored untill excreted via anus

Food allergies

Human ummune’s system abnormal response to a food protein. Different from intolerance. Food allergy, potential for anaphylactic reaction. Affect 4% of US population (12 millions US). 4% of kids < 18 years old have allergy

  • milk
  • egg
  • wheat
  • soy
  • peanut
  • tree nut (almond, cashew, pine nut, pecan, walnut)
  • fish
  • crustacean shellfish

Symptoms

  • tingling sensation in mouth
  • swelling of toungue and throat
  • difficulty breathing
  • hives, vomiting
  • abdominal cramps, diarrhea
  • drop in blood pressure
  • loss of consciosness
  • death

Diagnosing

  • skin prick test or blood test
  • test restuls compared to in-depth individual history

Prognosis

  • outgrow allergies to milk, egg, soy and wheat by 10 years old, often by 5 age
  • 85% of children with allergies to milk or egg will outgrow
  • all children with alergies to soy or wheat will outgrow
  • peanuts, tree nuts, fish and shellfish - lifelong allergies

FALCPA - food allergen labeling and consumer protection act. Took effect January 1, 2006
