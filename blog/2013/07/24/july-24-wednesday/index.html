Arguments for Affirmative Action:

  1. One argument has to do with correcting for the effects of educational disadvantage. Corrective - correcting for differences in educational backgrounds. That argument is consistent in principle with the idea that only academic promise and scholarly potential should count in admissions. We just need to go beyond test scores and grades alone to get a true estimate of academic promise and scholarly ability.

  2. Affirmative action is justified even where there may not be the need to correct for educational disadvantage in a particular applicant’s case. Compensatory - compensating for past wrongs.

  3. Diversity - (different from compensatory, because it makes a certain appeal to the social purpose or the social mission of the college or univesity) Two arguments:

  4. for educational experience for everyone

  1. automatically reconstruct the behaviors
  2. system call centric analysis
  3. android version independent
  4. dynamically stimulates apps to disclose additional behaviors

System calls on Linux ARM

Like on Intel, on ARM architecture invoking a system call induces a user-to-kernel transiction.

  • On ARM invoked through the swi instruction
  • r7 contains the number of the invoked syscall
  • r0-r5 contain parameters
  • lr contains the return address

System call analysis:

  • intercept when a syscall is invoked
  • we need to intercept return to user-space too!
  • there is no SYSEXIT/SYSRET to intercept
  • not every syscall actually returns to lr (exit, execve)

CopperDriod’s Approach:

  • instruments QEMU’s emulation of the swi instruction
  • instruments QEMU to intercept every cpsr_write (Kernel -> User)

CopperDroid VMI:

  • process name
  • pid and tid
  • process resources

when executing kernel code, the base of the stack points to the current executing thread.

Binder protocol is the core of Android IPC/RPC

  • intents are carried through binder
  • interactions with the system go through binder
  • binder driver enforces (some) permission policies

Application cannot send SMSs on their own, but must invoke (RPC) the proper system service to do that.

Plato with arguments for the immortality of the Soul

Suppose I pluck a string on my harp, producing a certain note. As we know, the vibrations of one string can set into play the other strings vibrating as well. That would be analogous to what goes on when my mind affects my body.

An essential property is a property that a given object must have; it always has as long as it exists at all.

A contingent property is a property that an object may have, may happen to have its entire existence, but could’ve existed without.

My car is blue (contingent property), it could be red, but the car will still exist. It could exist as a red car and even if I don’t change the color, still it could be red.

Pencil is whole, but I could break it (contingent property).

Fire and being hot, fire is hot, that’s property, it isn’t contingent property, it IS essential property!

Plato says ”Here is an essential property for the soul. Wherever there’s a soul it’s alive (it’s thinking, something capable of thought).”

  1. Life is an essential property of the soul.

  2. Soul is deathless.

  3. _Anything that’s deathless - cannot die, indestructible.

  4. Soul cannot be destroyed.

What does it mean to say that something is deathless?

  • it can’t be that soul exists AND dead
  • it can’t be that soul was destroyed

If life is an essential property of the soul, then that means we will never have soul in our hand and is dead.

How do you go about proving what something doesn’t exist? When do you need to prove that something doesn’t exist? Example with dragons, how do I prove that there aren’t any dragons? Debunk about dragons and unicorns, you don’t need to check all caves around the world to say “no dragons here, no dragons in there”, you are pretty much justified in being skeptical about the existence of dragons once you’ve undermined the arguments for dragons.

Aristotle explicitly ties justice to honor, honoring virtue and merit and moral desert.

For Aristotle, justice is a matter of giving people what they deserve, giving people their due. It’s a matter of figuring our the proper fir between persons with their virtues and their appropriate social roles.

Justice means giving each person his or her due, giving people what they deserve. But what is a person’s due? Aristotle says that depends on the sort of things being distributed.

Justice involves two factors: things and the persons to whom the things are assigned. In general we say that persons who are equal should have equal things assigned to them. Aristotle

Equals in what respect? That depends on the sort of thing we are distributing. Example with flutes and it’s distributing. What is the relevant merit or basis of desert for flutes? Who should get the best ones? Artistotle answers was - the best flute players. All justice involves districimination, he says, what matters is that the distrimination be according to the relevant excellence, according to the virtue appropriate to having flutes. He says, it would be unjust to discriminte on some other basis in giving our the flutes.
