Groundwork for the Metaphysics of Morals

Rational knowledge:

  • material - deteminate objects and laws to which they are subject.
  • formal - universal laws of thought in general without distinction of its objects. Logic. Logic can’t have any empirical part.

We must begin with pure philosophy (metaphysic), and without it there cannot be any moral philosphy at all.

empirical means based on, concerned with or verifiable by observation or experience rather than theory or pure logic.

We may call all philosophy empirical, so far as it is based on grounds of experience: on the other hand, that which deliviers its doctrines from a priori principles alone we may call pure philosophy. If it’s merely formal it is logic; if it is restricted to definite objects of the understanding - metaphysic.

For the metaphysic of morals has to examine the idea and the principles of a possible pure will, and not the acts and conditions of human volition generally, which for the most part are drawn from psychology.

Justice: The supreme principle of morality

When reason governs our will, we are not driven by the desire to seek pleasure and avoid pain. Our capacity for reason is bound up with our capacity for freedom. Taken together, these capacities make us distincive, and set us apart from mere animal existence. They make us more than mere creatures of appetite.

Whenever my behavior is biologically determined of socially conditioned, it is not truly free. To act freely, according to Kant, is to act autonomously. And to act autonomously is to act according to a law I give myself - not according to the dictates of nature or social convention.

What is the supreme (высший) principle of morality?

  1. MORALITY motive according to which we act. the motive of duty, doing the right thing for the right reason. For an action to have moral worth it must be done for the sake of duty not out of inclination

  2. FREEDOM determination of will. desire to persue some interest. Two different ways that my will can be determined:

  3. autonomously (according to Kant I’m only free when it determened autonomously which means it’s according to a law that I give myself. Where the law come from (a law that we give ourselves)? Reason, if reason determines my will then the real becomes to power to choose independent of the dictates of nature or inclination or circumstance)

  4. heteronously

  5. REASON Imperatives

  6. hypothetical imperatives use instrimental reason (if you want X then do y. It means ends reason, example if you want a good business reputation then don’t shortchange your customers)

  7. categorical withour reference to or dependents on any further purpose

If the action would be good solely as a means to something else, the imperative is hypothetical; if the action is represented as good in itself and therefore as necessary… for a will wchi of itself accords with reason, then the imperative is categorical. Immanuel Kant

The categorical imperative

  1. The Formula of Univesal Law. Act only on that maxim (the rule that explain the reason that you are doing, a principle, for example promise keeping) whereby you can at the same time will that is should become a universal law.

  2. The Formula of Humanity as End. We can’t base the categorical imperative on any particular interests, purposes, or ends because then it would be only relative to the person whose ends they were, but suppose there were something whose existence has in itself an absolute value… an end in itself… then in it, and in it alone, would there be the ground of a possible categorical imperative. I say that man, and in general every rational being, exists as an end in himself, not merely as a means for arbitraty use by this or that will.

Act in such a way that you always treat humanity, whether in your own person or in the person of any other, never simply as a means, but always at the same time, as an end. Immanuel Kant

Example: When I make a false promise to you, I mean using you as a means to my ends, to my desire for the hundred dollars and so I’m failing to respect you, your dignity, I’m manipulating you

Example with murder and suicide: they are odds with categorical imperative. Why? If I murder someone, I’m taking their life for some purpose (maybe I’m hires killer or I’m in the throws of some great anger or passion) as a means, so murder violate categorical imperative. With suicide we failing to respect humanity as and end, so I violate that dignity in my own person if I commit suicide and in murder if I take somebody else’s life from a moral point of view.

Kantian respect is unlike love in this way, it’s unlike sympathy, because love and other thing have to do with who they are in particular, but respect for Kant is respect for humanity which is universal.
