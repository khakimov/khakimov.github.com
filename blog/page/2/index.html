Subjective - is something that exists depending on the consciosness and beliefs of a single individual and it will disappear or change if that particular individual changes his or her beliefs. Exmaple: many children believe in the existence of imaginery friend that only the child himself can see and can talk to. And usually as a child grows up, he stops believing in this imaginary friend.

Inter-subjective - is something that exists within the communication network that link the subjective consciosness of many individuals. If a single individual changes his/her beliefs or even dies, it is of little importance. What are Arguments used for?

Persuading is making people believe or do something that they would not otherwise believe or do.

Justifying is showing someone a reason to believe the conclusion.

Justification tries to give good reasons. Persuasion tries to change your belief or actions (and bad reasons can do that).

When someone give you an argument, you need to ask a series of questions:

  1. Is the arguer trying to change someone’s mind? If so, the purpose is persuasion.

  2. Is the arguer trying to give some kind of reason to believe something? If so, the purpose is justification.
