Justice

Immanuel Kant

Not to use people as means even for good ends. Difficult thinker. The book is about what the supreme principle of morality is and it’s also gives us an account one of the most powerful accounts we have of what freedom really is.

Kant rejected utilitarism. He thinks that every human beings have a certain dignity (достоинство) that commands our respect. The resaon the individual is sacred or the bearer of right according to Kant, doesn’t stem from the idea that we own ourselves, but instead from the idea that we are all ratinal beings. Which means we are beings who are capable of reason. We also autonomous beings which is to say that we are beings capable of acting and choosing freely.

Kant admints the utilitarians were half right, we seek to avoid pain and we like plreasure. He does deny is Bentham’s clain that pain and pleasure are our sovereign (верховный) masters. Kant thinks that’s wrong, it’s rational capacity that makes us distinctive, that makes us special, that set us apart from and above mere animal existence. It makes us something more than just physical creatures with appetites.

Kant about freedom

When we like animals seek after pleasure or the satisfaction of our desires of the avoidance pain when we do that we arean’t really acting freely. We realy acting as the slaves of those appetites and impulses.

I didn’t choose this particular hunger or that particular appetite, and so when I act to satisfy it I’m just acting accorind to natural necessity and for Kant freedom is opposite of necessity.

obey your thirst - example with Sprite slogan. You think you choosing freely Sprite or Pepsi, but you’re actually obeying something, a thirst, or maybe a desire manufactured or messaged by advertising.

How can my will be detemined if not by prompting subnature or my hunger or my appetite or my desires? Kant’s conception of freedom

Autonomy

to act freely is to act autonomously = is to act according to a law that I give myself. (not according to the physical laws of nature or to the laws of cause and effect which include my desire, to eat or to drink or to choose this food in a restaurant over that)

Heteronomy

when I act heteronomously I’m acting according to an inclination (наклонность, склон, предрасположение) or a desire that I haven’t chosen for myself.

To act freely is not to choose the best means to a given end - it’s to choose the end itself for its own sake. (here is example with bilard ball which according to the law of nature/law of gravity will drop on the floor and can only follow this rules, when himan being can do - choose the end itself).

We act as means to the realization of ends given outside us, we are instruments rather than authors of the purposes we pursue. (heteronomy)

When we act as autonomus we cease to be instruments to purposes given outside us, we become or we can come to think of ourselves as ends in ourselves. (it gives humans special dignity)

respecting human dignity means regarding persons not just as means but also as end themselves (this is why it’s wrong to use people for the sake of other people well being of happiness). (this is why Kant thinks utilitarianism goes wrong, it’s important to respect the dignity of persons and to uphold (поддерживать) their rights). Freedom as autonomy.

What gives an action it’s moral worth? What makes an action morally worthy consists not in the conseqiences or in the results that flow from it - morally worthy has to do with the motive with the quality of the will with the intention for which the act is down and the motive must be of a certain kind.

Type 1

  • results from Betta-cell destruction, usually leading to absolute insulin deficiency
  • 5-10% of all cases of diabetes
  • IDDM or juvenile-onset diabetes

Type 2

  • results from progressive insulin secretory defect on background of insulin resistence
  • 90-95% of all cases
  • NIDDM or adult-onset diabetes

Diagnosis

Drink glucose and test blood in 2 hour (OGTT) > 200 mg/dL

Prediabetes

Don’t meet criteria for diabetes, high risk for future diabetes. Usually associated with obesity (abdominal or visceral), dyslepidemia. If OGTT test shows 140-199 mg/dL.

If BMI > 25 and one of from the list physical inactive, high-risk race/ethinicity, hypertension (blood pressure > 140/90).

recommendations for prediabetes:

  • 7% body weight loss
  • regular physical activity (150 min/week)
  • spread over at least 3 days/week
  • reduce calories and fat intake
  • target heart rate 50-70% (max rate = 220 - age in yr)

Physical Activity and Weight

Overweigh gain insulin resistance. 5% body weight loss associated with improved insulin sensetivity. Regular exercise has been shown to improve blood glucose control and well-being, reduce CVD risk factors and promote weight loss.

Glycemic Index and Glycemid Load

GI is rating system based on scale of 0 - 100, detemined experimentally (50 g carb and measure blood glucore response is measured). High-GI foods > 70, Low-GI < 55. If you eat High-GI food it will cause your blood sugar to spike more rapidly (higher blood sugar).

GL = divide GI of a food by 100 and then multiply by grams of carb in 1 serving.

Bypassing DEP/ASLR Protections

Today we are going to learn about data execution prevention (DEP) and address space layout randomization (ASLR). Last Sunday I read about return-into-libc and one week before was post about buffer overflow as-is. Both methods reqired to know the address of attacker shellcode and/or standard kernel library (we found out the address of system() function in the memory to use it in return-into-libc with this code int main() { system();}). Modern systems decided to implement something what will make stack addresses and addressess of library routines unpredictable and different from machine to machine (no more universal exploits =) kidding).

Author Dionysus Blazakis holds BS from University of Maryland and doing interesting things, I saw his presentation “The Apple Sandbox” and then found this paper, great job Dionysus. I inspired by his example and it motivates me to keep digging in research field. Dion, if you’ll ever read this I just want to say thank you! Lets get back to his paper and read what this smart fellow prepared for us.

Blazakis

DEP and ASLR are very real problem in the side of an exploit developer. DEP makes locating shellcode difficult, ASLR further complicates an attack by obfuscating the base address of the loaded data. Adobe Flash Player is used as an example, Dion also referenced to JIT spraying (Just-In-Time).

JIT spraying is very simular to heap spraying, in simple terms attacker stuff heap with multiple copies of attack code (payload) and that increases the chances of successful exploitation.

JIT Spray Exploit Demo

So, why do you need this? If you found the overflow in the modern browsers you will not able to use it easily, because of randomization, the address space is randomized and you don’t know where to point EIP and even if you have data in the heap DEP wont allow you to execute it. But you can use Just-In-Time compiler which will allocate writable memory for you and write executable code into it. For more details please read the paper above. Enjoy!
