Arguments Markers

I am tall, and I am good at sport.

vs

I am tall, so I am good at sports.

When you use the word and, you’re simply stating the two facts. But when you use the word so (therefore, thus, hence, accordingly), you’re indicating that one of them is a reson for the other.

Kinds of argument markers:

Conclusion markers: so, therefore, thus, accordingly, they indicate that the sentece right after them is a conclusion.

After the words because, for, as, for the reason that, and the reason why are a reason or a premise. And we going to call it a reason marker, or a premise marker.
